# Design Decisions

## Why a mobile app?

This technology is highly convenient for the end user. The likelihood is that in a friendship group at least one person has a mobile device on them. 
One phone with this app downloaded enough for them to play together.


## Why Android?

Since the mobile app is free, it’s better to publish on a platform with low publishing costs. Google Play Store requires a one time $25 fee for developers. 
In contrast, the iOS App Store requires $99/year.  Additionally, publishing an app on the Google Play Store is a much simpler process than for the iOS App Store.
Android will be the initial platform, but as time progresses, the app may be made available on iOS.


## Why Offline?

Internet access is simply not necessary for this game. Since the target audience is friendship groups, the user benefits from offline face-to-face interactions as this complements 
the social deduction element of the game. An online version would be technically complex, inaccessible to some users, and would limit the quality of social interactions between players.


## Why Python?

Most modern mobile apps use either Flutter or React Native. So why use Python? As a developer, I already have intermediate experience with Python. 
I’d like to upskill my Python as it still remains a highly versatile and in-demand programming language. Python with Kivy is the fastest path to a working Android app given my current skill level.


## Why the Kivy Framework?

Kivy was one of the few cross-platform GUI frameworks with a large community and clear documentation. 
An additional benefit is that Kivy GUI can be polished using KivyMD to ensure there is a high quality Material Design.


## Why Open Source?

This allows for the project’s source code to be under the scrutiny of other programmers. 
Contributions to the app are welcome and community trust is built as the inner workings of the app are not black-boxed.


## Why Free?

I would like this app to be accessible to a wide audience. In app purchases may create a divide between this audience, as not everyone is able to pay for features. 
Ads are generally undesirable and this project is not associated with them. Therefore, donations will be the source of income, keeping the app free.
